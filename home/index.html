who I am

A person who takes things seriously and nothing too seriously.

I'm Duc Anh — a researcher working at the intersection of energy systems and public policy in Southeast Asia. My day job is figuring out how countries can transition away from fossil fuels without leaving people behind.

Outside of work, I read too much, travel when I can, and think about the relationship between technology, institutions, and human flourishing. I'm drawn to ideas that don't sit comfortably in one discipline.

This site is my corner of the internet — a place to share what I'm thinking, reading, and making. It's personal in the truest sense.

how I show up

Wired, not performed.

Obsessively curious

I follow threads until they run out. Energy policy led me to political philosophy. A novel sent me down a rabbit hole about Kyoto School Buddhism. I don't fight the tangents.

Quietly intense

I tend to be still in groups and loud in one-on-ones. I listen more than I speak, but when I have something to say, I've usually been thinking about it for a while.

Systems thinker

I notice patterns and feedback loops before I notice individual actors. Useful in policy work. Sometimes annoying in normal conversation.

Write to think

Writing isn't how I communicate ideas I've already formed. It's how I form them. Most of my best thinking happens at a keyboard, not before.

Earnestly sincere

Irony is fine in small doses. I'd rather mean what I say. I find cynicism lazy and sincerity underrated. This site is an artifact of that.

Rituals person

Matcha in the morning. Long walks with podcasts. Reading before bed. Routine is how I stay grounded when work is chaotic, which is often.

what I promise

Three things I believe. Three things I'll hold to.

These aren't values I've chosen for their convenience. They're the ones that keep returning even when I push them away.
