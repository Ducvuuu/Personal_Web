Grand strategy games

Hearts of Iron 4, EU4, Victoria 3. Managing ideology, alliances, and industrial capacity at a continental scale. Less gaming, more thinking-through-playing. History as a system you can poke.

City building

Cities: Skylines 2. I work in urban-adjacent policy by day, and apparently need to also do it for fun. There's something about designing how people move through space that I can't leave alone.

The writing, specifically

I write slowly, seriously, and mostly for myself — though I'm learning to share it.

The Third Arch

A prose triptych — three movements, one story. It's a myth I rewrote from the inside, filtering the Artemis archetype through something that actually happened to me. I'm prouder of it than almost anything else I've made. It's the kind of piece you can only write by writing it, not by planning it.

The Spratly Gambit

A geopolitical thriller set in near-future Southeast Asia, following a Vietnamese junior diplomat navigating a crisis over rare earth minerals in disputed waters. Part political fiction, part love letter to the region, part me working out how I feel about duty and conscience and the cost of staying in the room.

Still being written. Probably always will be.

I write because some things can only be said sideways. Fiction is the sideways.
